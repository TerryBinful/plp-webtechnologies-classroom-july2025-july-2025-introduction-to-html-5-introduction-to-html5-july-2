Introduction

Setting up a desktop computer involves connecting several essential peripherals to the system unit. This guide will help you identify the correct ports and make the right connections for a smooth setup experience.

Connecting the Monitor

  • Locate the VGA, DVI, HDMI, or DisplayPort on the back of your system unit.
  • Connect the monitor cable to the appropriate port.
  • Plug in the monitor's power cable and turn it on.

Connecting the Keyboard

  • Find the USB or PS/2 port on your system unit.
  • Insert the keyboard connector into the port.
  • Most modern keyboards use USB and are plug-and-play.

Connecting the Mouse

  • Locate a USB port (or PS/2 port for older mice) on the system unit.
  • Plug the mouse into the port.
  • Wait for the system to recognize the device.

Connecting the Speakers

  • Find the green audio-out (headphone) jack on the back of the system unit.
  • Connect the speaker cable to this jack.
  • Plug in the speaker's power supply if needed and turn them on.
